03

Setting Up

1. Any table works. Objects on it become terrain.
2. Each player takes one edge. Deploy within 6" of your edge.
3. Place 3 Prize Tokens in the middle, at least 6" apart and 6" from each deployment zone.
4. Shuffle the Gear deck. Each player draws an opening hand of 5 cards. v9
5. Both roll a d20. Highest wins Turn 1 initiative. Reroll ties.
6. Alternate placing models one at a time. Initiative player places first.
🩸 Starting Blood
  • Scrapper — 4
  • Brute — 7
  • Big Bozza — see card
💀 KO'd

A model at 0 blood tokens is immediately removed from the table.

✋ Opening Hand

Both players draw a flat 5 cards regardless of squad size — leaner crews get a fuller start.

04

Core Concepts

👊 Engaged

Within 1" of an enemy. Affects Retaliation and Fist Fights. A model may be engaged with more than one enemy.

👁 Line of Sight

Trace a straight line attacker to target. Blocked by models and terrain taller than the model.

🛡 Cover

Any object between attacker and defender. +4 to defender's Defence value. Same object counts for Cover and Scavenge.

Turn Structure

1
Order Phase
Return all model cards to upright.
Gain 1 Order Token per model on the table, including BB.
Place a Command Token coin beside your BB's card (if alive).

Both players bid one card face down. Reveal simultaneously. Highest tip value wins. Both discarded.

No bids: holder keeps. Tied: player who did NOT have it last turn wins.

Drawing & Hand Limit v9

Your card income now scales with your surviving crew — draw 1 per model each Order Phase. Wide squads run a card engine; lean squads rely on big swings and Scavenge.

Hand limit: 7. You may exceed 7 during your turn, but in the End Phase you must discard down to 7.

Command Token

Generated by your BB. May be spent during the Action Phase to activate any friendly model — even one already activated this turn.

Each model may only benefit from one Command Token activation per turn, unless a specific ability states otherwise. If your BB is KO'd, no Command Token is generated for the rest of the game.

2
Action Phase

The initiative player decides who activates first. Players alternate. Spend 1 Order Token → activate a model → rotate card sideways → take your Move + Action (see Section 06).

Passing: No tokens or choose not to activate. Phase ends when both pass consecutively.

3
End Phase
1. Claim Prize Tokens. Any unengaged model within 1" of a Prize Token claims it — score 3 points, remove the token. v9
2. Check for victory.
3. Discard down to 7 cards. v9
06

Actions

⚡ Your Activation v9 — the big change

Spend 1 Order Token to activate a model. It may Move up to its Speed AND take ONE Action, and may play ONE buff / Free Action card on top.

Your Action is ONE of:

  • Fist Fight an engaged enemy, or
  • play one Order card, or
  • use your BB's Active ability.

You cannot Fist Fight and play an Order card — they share the one Action slot. Reactions and Desperate Acts are separate and may still be played at their proper timing (a Desperate Act may stack on top of your one buff card — that's your Hail-Mary lane).

07

Combat

Must be engaged (within 1") to make a Fist Fight. One rule governs every die, attack or defence: roll exactly the value = Crit, roll a natural 1 = automatic fail.

⚔️🛡 Combat Resolution v9

Step 1 — Attack. Roll your attack dice. Each die:

  • Exactly your Skill → CRIT: deals 2 damage; can only be stopped by a Crit Block.
  • 2 up to your Skill → Hit: deals 1 damage.
  • Natural 1 → Miss.

Step 2 — Defence. Roll one defence die per landed attack die (Hit or Crit), plus any bonus dice from cards or stance. Each die:

  • Exactly your Defence → CRIT BLOCK: stops 2 damage — it can cancel a Crit, or mop up two Hits.
  • 2 up to your Defence → Block: stops 1 Hit. (A normal Block cannot touch a Crit.)
  • Natural 1 → Fail.

Step 3 — Resolve. Assign blocks to the defender's advantage. Net damage = everything left unblocked. You can never block more damage than was dealt. The defender loses that much Blood.

🎯 Worked Example

Brute (Skill 14, 3 dice) rolls 14, 9, 1 → one Crit (2 dmg) + one Hit (1 dmg) + a miss = 3 raw, 2 landed dice.

Scrapper (Def 10) rolls 2 defence dice: 10, 6 → a Crit Block + a Block. The Crit Block cancels the Crit; the Block stops the Hit. Net: 0 — a perfect parry!

👥 Gang Up v9

Scrappers roll +1 attack die per friendly Scrapper within 4", max +2. Applies only to a model's primary Fist Fight — never to free, extra, or Reaction Fist Fights.

Retaliation

When an engaged model moves away or plays a ranged card while engaged, every engaged enemy makes a free Fist Fight first.

  • Does not cost Orders. Resolves before the provoking action.
  • If the provoker is KO'd, their action is cancelled.
  • If the provoker is KO'd mid-Retaliation, all remaining retaliations from that trigger cancel.
  • Retaliation cannot trigger further Retaliation. Gang Up does not apply (it's not a primary Fist Fight).

Ranged Attacks v9

Only via Gear cards. Require Line of Sight.

  • You may not ranged-attack an enemy you are engaged with — throw fists, not bottles.
  • You may target a different, unengaged enemy, but this provokes Retaliation from everyone engaging you.
  • The +1 base attack die never applies to ranged cards — they roll exactly the dice printed.

Losing vs. Spending Blood

Losing = damage from attacks or cards. Spending = using a Desperate Act. Both reduce your count. At 0 — KO'd, removed from table.

🩸 Becoming Bloodied

Big Bozzas: Flip card to Bloodied side permanently.

Scrappers & Brutes: Track via token count. Bloodied effects apply at half Blood or below.

🔥 Bloodied = Unlock

When Bloodied, your Desperate Acts switch to their enhanced versions, and you may perform two Desperate Acts per activation. Getting hurt makes you dangerous.

09

Desperate Acts v9 — rebuilt

Three clean gambles — one for Moving, one for Hitting, one for Blocking. All are paid in blood, sit outside your Action slot, and may stack on top of a buff card.

The Rules of Desperation
  • Cost is paid in blood. Minimum cost 1.
  • A Desperate Act can never reduce you below 1 blood — you cannot KO yourself.
  • One per activation — two if you are Bloodied.
  • Bloodied isn't a discount; it's an unlock to the enhanced effect.

⚠️ Self-KO Scoring

Points for KO'ing a model are awarded to the opponent regardless of cause — including BB area effects. (A model can never KO itself by spending blood on a Desperate Act.)

Victory Conditions

⚡ Immediate Win

All enemy models KO'd at any point — you win immediately.

🏆 After 4 Turns

Player with most points wins.

Tiebreakers (in order)

  1. Whose BB is still alive
  2. Whose BB has more blood remaining
  3. If still tied — draw
Concurrent Triggers

When two effects would happen at the same time, the player with initiative chooses which resolves first.

Tuning Dials Playtest Appendix

Pre-agreed levers — not in the base v9 rules. Reach for these only if the relevant problem shows up in play, so you're not redesigning at the table.

🛡 Brute feels like a piñata?

Dial: Brutes roll a permanent 2nd defence die (still Def 6). Adds the "I'm tanking" feel without turning the Brute into a dodge-tank. Only raise the Def number (to 8) if even this isn't enough.

💥 Hail-Mary burst too swingy?

Dial: Hard cap — "no single attack roll may exceed 6 dice." Crude but bulletproof. The per-hit defence system should already tame most of this; try without first.

🥊 Stand Your Ground + DA stacking

By default these stack (a Scrapper can plant for +1 die and Come On Then!). Dial: if that combo feels oppressive, rule that Stand Your Ground's bonus does not stack with a Desperate Act on the same swing.

🃏 Card hoarding (Barfly/Grifter)?

The 7-card End-Phase discard should handle it. Dial: if a player still turtles into a giant hand, lower the cap to 6, or cap Order-Phase draw at 4/turn.

🔭 The 3 Things To Watch This Playtest
  • Does Move + Act fix "turn 1 = nothing"? (the whole point of v9)
  • Is the per-hit defence + symmetric crit fun and fast, or fiddly?
  • Are Brutes satisfying now, or do they need the 2nd defence die?
