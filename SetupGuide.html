Know Your Goal
Game lasts 4 turns — or ends instantly if a whole squad is KO'd.
Score points by claiming Prizes and knocking out enemies.
Most points after Turn 4 wins. Protect your BB — lose it and you lose your Command Token & archetype bonuses!

🔄 How a Turn Works — repeat 4 times

1 · Order Phase
Stand all model cards upright.
Take 1 Order Token per model.
Put a Command Token by your BB.
Draw 1 card per model.
Turn 2+: bid a card for initiative.
2 · Action Phase
Alternate activating models.
Spend 1 Order Token → activate 1 model → it Moves + Acts (see below).
Phase ends when both players pass.
3 · End Phase
Claim Prizes: unengaged model within 1" → +3 pts.
Check for a winner.
Discard down to 7 cards.

⚡ Your Activation — the heart of the game

When you activate a model, it does all of this:

MOVE up to Speed + ONE Action + ONE Buff / Free card

You always move AND take an Action — they're separate. Your ONE Action is a single choice from:

  • Fist Fight an engaged enemy (within 1"), or
  • Play 1 Order card (ranged attack, heal, etc.), or
  • Use your BB's Active ability.

You can't Fist Fight and play an Order card — pick one. But you may always layer one buff/Free card on top, and play Reactions & Desperate Acts when their moment comes. Touching Clutter? Scavenge free once a turn (draw 2, keep 1).

🥊 Fist Fight in 3 Steps
  • ① Attack: roll your attack dice. Each die ≤ Skill = a Hit (1 dmg). Roll exactly Skill = a CRIT (2 dmg!). A natural 1 always misses.
  • ② Defence: defender rolls 1 die per Hit/Crit that landed (+ any bonus dice). Each ≤ Defence = blocks 1. Exactly Defence = CRIT BLOCK (blocks 2 / stops a Crit).
  • ③ Damage: whatever's left through = Blood lost. At 0 Blood → KO'd.
💥 Good to Know
  • Bloodied (half Blood or below): your Desperate Acts get nastier and you can use two a turn.
  • Gang Up: Scrappers get +1 attack die per friendly Scrapper within 4" (max +2).
  • Cover (+4 Def) and positioning win fights — use the clutter.
  • Don't shoot someone you're engaged with — and moving away from a fight lets them swing first (Retaliation).
